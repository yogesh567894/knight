Frequently asked questions

Answers to most common questions.

What should I do after 12th grade?

After 12th grade, you can pursue higher education, such as a bachelor's degree, or explore vocational courses, certifications, or apprenticeships in fields that interest you. Your choice should align with your career goals and interests.

How do I choose the right college or university?

Research colleges or universities based on your preferred location, courses, financial constraints, and future career prospects. Consider factors like accreditation, faculty, infrastructure, and student reviews to make an informed decision.

What are some popular career options after 12th grade?

Popular career options include engineering, medical, commerce, arts, computer science, and more. However, it's essential to choose a career that matches your interests and strengths, rather than just following trends.

How can I apply for scholarships or financial aid?

Research scholarships and financial aid opportunities offered by colleges, government, or private organizations. Meet the application deadlines, provide necessary documents, and follow the guidelines to maximize your chances of securing financial assistance.

How do I build a strong resume for college applications or job searches?

A strong resume should highlight your achievements, skills, and experiences. Include your academic performance, extracurricular activities, volunteer work, and any relevant part-time jobs or internships.

What's the best way to prepare for college or job interviews?

Interview preparation involves practicing common interview questions, researching the organization or college, dressing appropriately, and improving your communication and interpersonal skills. Mock interviews can also be beneficial.
